5K Outdoor Run · or Indoor Cycle if wet
Head outside when weather allows — fresh air and varied terrain make this more enjoyable and better for cadence development. If it's raining or too cold, swap to 30–40 min on the exercise bike at moderate effort. Start comfortable, build gradually. Focus on cadence — aim for 160–165 spm. Keep your upper body relaxed and strike mid-foot.

🧘 Vinyasa — how to approach it

Set Down Dog to medium or slow flow pace. Focus on matching breath to movement — exhale into Chaturanga, inhale to Upward Dog. Keep your core lightly engaged throughout. Don't rush transitions; the breath sets the tempo.

01
Kettlebell Goblet Squat
16kg KB + Heel Wedges Quads · Glutes · Core 3 × 10–12
▾
  • 1Hold 16kg KB by the horns at chest height, elbows tucked
  • 2Place heel wedges under heels to compensate for tight calves
  • 3Feet shoulder-width, toes out 20–30°
  • 4Push hips back, descend with chest up and spine neutral
  • 5Hit parallel or deeper — elbows slide inside knees
  • 6Drive through full foot, squeeze glutes at the top
Cue: "Elbows to thighs, chest up" — keeps you upright and deep without folding forward.

Kettlebell Deadlift
2 × 7.5kg KBs Hamstrings · Glutes · Lower Back 3 × 10
▾
  • 1Place both 7.5kg KBs on the floor, one either side of your feet
  • 2Hinge at hips — push them back, slight bend in the knee
  • 3Grip handles, shoulders pulled back and down
  • 4Drive through heels, squeeze glutes as you stand tall
  • 5Hinge back down with control — don't let the back round
  • 6Reset at the bottom before each rep
Cue: "Push the floor away" not "pull the weight up" — keeps the movement in the legs and glutes, not the back.

03
Resistance Band Glute Bridge
Band + 7.5kg KB Glutes · Hip Abductors 3 × 15
▾
  • 1Loop band just above your knees
  • 2Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat hip-width apart
  • 3Rest 7.5kg KB on your hips for added load
  • 4Press knees out against the band throughout
  • 5Drive hips up until shoulders-to-knees form a straight line
  • 6Squeeze hard at the top, hold 1 sec, lower with control
Cue: Don't let knees cave inward — push out against the band the whole time. This fires the glute med and builds hip stability.

04
Weighted Step-Up
Step + 2 × 1kg DBs Quads · Glutes · Balance 3 × 10 each leg
▾
  • 1Stand in front of step, 1kg dumbbell in each hand
  • 2Place right foot fully on the step, knee over toes
  • 3Drive through the right heel to step up — don't push off the back foot
  • 4Bring left foot up, stand tall at the top
  • 5Lower left foot down with control, then right
  • 6Complete all reps on one side, then switch
Cue: The trailing leg should do almost nothing — all the drive comes from the front leg. If you're pushing off the floor, slow down.

05
Kettlebell Swing
16kg KB Glutes · Hamstrings · Core 3 × 15
▾
  • 1Stand feet shoulder-width, KB on floor in front
  • 2Hinge at hips, grip handle with both hands
  • 3Hike KB back between your legs like hiking a football
  • 4Explosively drive hips forward — let momentum float KB to chest height
  • 5At the top: stand tall, glutes squeezed, core braced
  • 6Let it swing back, hinge — not squat — and repeat
Cue: Power comes from the hips, not the arms. Think "hip hinge" not "squat". Arms are just guiding the weight.

🌿 Yin — how to approach it

Completely passive — let gravity do the work. Target 6–8 poses, 3–5 minutes each. Don't fidget or micro-adjust. Your mind will want to escape — this is normal. Stay and breathe into the sensation. The savasana at the end is essential: Yin works deeply and the nervous system needs time to integrate.

🏃
5K Interval Run · or Interval Cycle if wet
After Thursday's full rest you should feel fresh — use it. This is your intensity day. Outdoor: Warm up 5 min easy, then alternate 1 min hard / 2 min easy for 5–6 rounds, cool down 5 min easy. Total ~30–35 min. On the bike: Same structure — 5 min warm up, 1 min at high resistance / 2 min easy spin, 5–6 rounds, 5 min cool down. The hard efforts should feel like a 7–8 out of 10 — uncomfortable but controlled.
⏱ 5 min warm-up · 5–6 × (1 min hard / 2 min easy) · 5 min cool-down · ~30–35 min total

🌀 Core Pilates — how to approach it

Lateral breathing is key — breathe into your ribcage sideways, not your belly, so your core stays braced. Think about initiating every movement from deep in the abdomen. Slow eccentrics (the return phase) are where most of the benefit happens — resist rather than collapse.

25–30 min session 3–5 min savasana Quality over reps
